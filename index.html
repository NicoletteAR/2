Lead In Mycological Organization

December 2022 to Present

What I do as a mycological group organizer. Leading a mycological group is one of the most fulfilling things I do. It brings together my love for fungi, community, education, and the outdoors. I’m not just organizing walks, I’m building a space for connection, curiosity, and stewardship.

• Build Community - create a welcoming, inclusive environment where people from all backgrounds and experience levels feel comfortable. Whether someone is a total beginner or a seasoned forager, I encourage questions, sharing, and learning. I also reach out to local schools, nature centers, and organizations to grow our community and inspire others to explore the fungal world.

• Organize Events - plan and lead mushroom walks, ID workshops, and educational talks. I scout locations with rich biodiversity, ensure we have the right permissions, and guide people through the woods with safety and purpose. After events, I help identify finds, encourage documentation, and guide people in uploading observations to platforms like iNaturalist or Mushroom.

• Observer - My group doesn’t just explore, we document. I photograph and log fungi, help others learn how to ID them, and sometimes collect specimens for herbaria or DNA sequencing projects. Our contributions support fungal biodiversity data and connect citizen science with professional research.

• Prioritize Safety and Ethics I model- and teach ethical foraging practices, respecting the land, collecting responsibly, and following local laws. I also make sure everyone is prepared and safe in the field. This includes carrying a first aid kit, giving clear guidance, and making sure people know how to avoid toxic or endangered species.

• Handle Communication and Coordination, manage our group’s calendar, social media, and outreach. I send out updates, answer questions, and coordinate with volunteers and guest experts. Keeping everyone informed and engaged is a big part of what makes the group thrive.

• Celebrate the Cultural Side of Fungi-Fungi aren’t just organisms to me, they’re part of the story, art, myth, and food. I love organizing events that celebrate that richness, from mushroom-themed potlucks and cooking demos to art nights and folklore talks. It brings a deeper human connection to what we do.

• Grow Through This Work- This work keeps me grounded. I’ve become more attuned to the seasons, more observant in nature, and more connected to the ecosystems around me. I’ve learned how fungi hold the forest together, and how community, like a mycelial network, flourishes when we share and support each other.
